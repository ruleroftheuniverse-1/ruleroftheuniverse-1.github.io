Research

My research blends conceptual analysis with systems modeling, drawing from philosophy, neuroscience, artificial intelligence, and math. I work across academic and practical boundaries to explore questions of identity, cognition, feedback, and emergence.

Ongoing Areas

  • Mathematical foundations of selfhood and recursive agency
  • Cognitive feedback system modeling
  • Personal identity and dissociation
  • AI alignment through formal models of value coherence
  • Information flow in epistemic networks

Selected Works

  • Between the Grid and the Ghost: A speculative metaphysical inquiry into the structure of consciousness and pattern.
  • Simulating Minds: A project on building a visualizable simulator for feedback-based cognitive agents.
  • Ontological Personalism and Control: A paper draft exploring the metaphysical grounding of agency and choice.
